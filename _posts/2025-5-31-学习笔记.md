## 容易忘记的语法

### 이에요 예요

- Use **이에요** after a consonant.
- Use **예요** after a vowel.

### 이 가 subject maker

  
Used to:

- Shows **who** or **what** is doing something.
- Often introduces **new information**.
- **Emphasizes** the subject itself.


[Subject + 이/가] + [Verb or adjective]

- **이** is used after a consonant
- **가** is used after a vowel

#### compare

| Particle | Use When                                                           |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 이/가      | You want to say who/what is doing something or being **described** |
| 은/는      | You want to **highlight** the topic or show contrast/comparison    |

### 은 는 topic maker

Used to:

- **Introduce** or **highlight** the topic of the sentence
- Show **contrast** or **comparison**
- Talk about things in a **general sense**

- 은 is used after a consonant
- 는 is used after a vowel

### 을 를 action maker

 [Subject] + [Object + 을/를] + [Verb]

- 을 is used after a consonant
- 를 is used after a vowel

###   으로 / 로 

Meaning:

- “to” (direction)
- “with” or “by” (means/tool)
- “as” (identity/role)
- “because of” (reason)

| Noun ends in         | Use this particle | Example                    |
| -------------------- | ----------------- | -------------------------- |
| Consonant (except ㄹ) | 으로                | 칼으로 (with a knife)         |
| Vowel or final ㄹ     | 로                 | 버스로 (by bus), 길로 (by road) |
